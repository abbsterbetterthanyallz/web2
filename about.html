Abigail Whitney is an award-winning stage director, artist educator and youth mentor with Soulpepper Theatre Company based in Toronto. She's trained across Toronto with professionals from The Randolph Academy of Performing Arts, The National Voice Intensive and at The University of Toronto Drama Centre, Theatre and Performance Studies. Whitney has worked with Djanet Sears, Deanna Bowen, Tanisha Taitt, Ngozi Paul, Anique Jordan, Andrea Nann, Paula Wing, Courtney Ch'ng Lancaster (at Tarragon Theatre General Auditions), Juno nominated director Emma Higgins, rising award-winning screenwriter and playwright Veronika Gribanova and Alex Bulmer, named one of the most influential disabled artists by UK Power Magazine. Whitney's been an assistant director to Ross Manson, Artistic Director of Volcano Theatre in Hannah Moscovitch's Infinity that toured British Columbia in January 2020 to much acclaim; and she's assistant directed Mumbi Tindyebwa-Otu, the standing Artistic Director of Obsidian Theatre and Leah Cherniak in the 2020 Dora nominated Here are the Fragments produced by The Theatre Centre. She's won Best Director at The UofT Drama Coalition for her directorial premiere of Les Freres in 2018 and Audience Choice Award in 2020 at The UofT Drama Festival for her past work. Whitney will be assistant directing Mike Payette in Lawrence Hill's monodrama for Obsidian Theatre's 2021 "21 Black Futures Project".
